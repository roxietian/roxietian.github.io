C · STAR
12AI hotline rolloutleadership · change · conflict
S&T → Hook → Action → Result → Lesson
S&T
at Autel, led the rollout of a multilingual AI hotline across 7 European markets
Hook
hardest part wasn't building the AI — it was getting 7 local teams to help make it reliable enough to launch
Problem
each local team had to validate the knowledge content in its own language — busy with daily support, not part of their targets → pushed down the list; without their input, couldn't guarantee answer quality → rollout stalling
Action
first understood their concerns → changed the message + the plan: "not reviewing content — building a system that reduces your own repetitive tickets"; started with 3 more engaged markets; gave local experts ownership of specific knowledge areas; short feedback loop (local ↔ product ↔ HQ)
Result
first 3 markets proved the value — ~50% automation; remaining teams much more willing → rolled out all 7
Lesson
change leadership starts with understanding why people resist, then making the value real for them
13CRM ecosystem 0→10-1 · uncertain · stakeholder
S&T → Hook → Action → Result → Lesson
S&T
build a CRM-based service ecosystem from scratch for major EV charging clients — every client: different SLA models, service packages, permission structures, reporting needs; no single person had the full picture
Hook
no mature CRM infrastructure in our European business, no industry benchmark — not improving an existing model, I had to create one
Action
researched competitors → their O&M also immature, nothing to copy; didn't wait for requirements — interviewed clients + internal teams, studied the contractual logic, mapped standard vs configurable; aligned commercial + service + dev on one direction: standard core workflow + flexible SLA rules, service tiers, permission-based access; chose flexibility over speed-to-launch (rigid → endless exceptions)
Result
200+ enterprise clients, 3,000+ verified users; became a benchmark used in RFPs; manager amazed by how much I drove independently
Lesson
in a zero-to-one project, you don't wait for the benchmark — sometimes you build it
